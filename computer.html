Computer Technology

Computer technology courses provide students with a foundation in how computers work and how they are used in various industries. These courses cover topics such as hardware, software, and programming. Students gain hands-on experience by building computers, writing code, troubleshooting issues, and exploring cybersecurity and data management. Through these activities, they develop problem-solving, logical thinking, and technical skills that are valuable in both everyday life and future careers. Computer technology courses also help students explore pathways in information technology, software development, and related fields.

Projects

Cyborg Mask

During this unit, I worked with a partner for the Cyborg Mask assignment, where we designed and built a functional cyborg mask and control system. The goal was to guide a blindfolded "cyborg" through a classroom maze using only LED signals controlled by switches on a handheld board. We researched electrical circuits, experimented with LED placement and signaling methods, and constructed both the mask and controller using cardboard, wires, resistors, and a 9V battery. We tested and refined our design to ensure reliable communication through light signals alone. The project challenged us to apply skills in electronics, design, teamwork, and problem-solving.
