Technological Design

Technological design courses in Ontario high schools introduce students to the creative and problem-solving processes involved in designing products, structures, and systems. These courses encourage innovation by having students plan, sketch, model, and test their ideas using both traditional tools and digital software. Through hands-on projects and teamwork, students learn how to think critically, communicate design concepts, and apply technology to real-world challenges. The courses help prepare students for future studies or careers in fields like engineering, architecture, or industrial design by developing both technical skills and creative thinking.

Projects

Egg Drop Challenge

During this unit, I completed the Egg Drop Device Challenge, where I designed and built a protective structure to prevent a raw egg from breaking when dropped from increasing heights. We could only use limited materials which were paper, straws, masking tape, and a Ziplock bag. Beforehand, we researched physics concepts such as gravity, drag, and impact force mitigation. After researching principles of aerodynamics and observing various design ideas, I constructed and tested several prototypes to determine the most effective design. Throughout the drop stages, I analyzed what worked and made adjustments to improve stability and shock absorption. This hands-on project helped me deepen my understanding of engineering design and the practical application of physics in problem-solving.

Homestyler

Another assignment that we did during this unit was the Tiny House Design assignment using Homestyler to create a detailed floor plan and interior layout for a 20' x 26' open-concept tiny house. I began by drawing a floor plan sketch on graph paper, ensuring all walls, windows, doors, and room dimensions were accurately represented. Using Homestyler, I then built this sketch into a digital model. We also had to place furniture, fixtures, and appliances to maximize functionality and aesthetic appeal within the small space. I carefully selected a cohesive colour palette and materials to create visual harmony throughout the design. This assignment helped me better understand space planning, architectural principles, and interior design.
