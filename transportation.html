Transportation Technology

Transportation technology courses give students a solid introduction to how vehicles and transportation systems work, from basic maintenance to understanding vehicle ownership. Students explore topics like engine fundamentals, safety practices, environmental impacts, and the social dimensions of owning and operating cars, trucks, or small engines. Through hands-on classwork, they learn practical skills. These courses help students discover career pathways in the automotive and transportation fields, prepare for apprenticeship programs or further studies, and raise awareness about the role of transportation in society.

Projects

MTO's Driver's Handbook Presentation

During this unit, my group completed a presentation based on Section 7 of the Ontario Driver's Licence Handbook, which focused on "Keeping Your Driver's Licence." We explored the responsibilities drivers must uphold to maintain their licence, including the legal and safety consequences of driving infractions, accumulating demerit points, and impaired driving. Our presentation aimed to clearly explain the actions that could lead to a licence suspension or revocation and why it is important to drive safely and responsibly at all times. Using slides and a blooket game, we taught our classmates how poor driving decisions can impact not only their legal standing but also their long-term freedom and job opportunities.
